# Employee-Attrition-Workforce-Analytics-Dashboard
Employee Retention &amp; HR Analytics Dashboard | Power BI | DAX | Power Query

## Business Problem

Employee attrition directly impacts organizational productivity, recruitment costs, and workforce planning. HR departments require actionable insights into employee demographics, compensation, work-life balance, tenure, and career progression to identify the key drivers of employee turnover.
This dashboard enables HR managers to monitor workforce trends, analyze attrition patterns, and make informed decisions to improve employee retention.

 Project Objective

- Develop an interactive Power BI dashboard that enables HR stakeholders to:
- Monitor overall workforce health
- Identify high-risk employee segments
- Analyze attrition across different demographics
- Evaluate employee tenure and career progression
- Support data-driven retention strategies

Tools Used

- Power BI
- Power Query
- DAX
- Data Modeling
- Microsoft Excel (Dataset)


## Dashboard Overview

### Executive Summary


<img width="866" height="486" alt="Screenshot 2026-07-08 123414" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4096a3cf-460e-47e6-a2c6-f57008e95be7" />

The dashboard tracks the following key performance indicators (KPIs):

- Employee Count – Total number of employees in the organization.
- Active Employees – Employees currently working in the organization (excluding attrition).
- Attrition Count – Total number of employees who have left the organization.
- Average Years in Company – The average tenure of employees, providing insight into workforce stability and retention.

To help identify the factors influencing employee turnover, the dashboard also visualizes the relationship between attrition and several key workforce characteristics using a combination of bar charts, pie charts, and trend visualizations, including:

- Salary Bands
- Age Groups
- Educational Background
- Employee Tenure in the Current Role

These visualizations enable HR professionals to quickly identify high-risk employee segments, recognize workforce trends, and support data-driven decisions aimed at improving employee retention and organizational planning.


### Employee Work-Life Balance Analysis

<img width="868" height="486" alt="Screenshot 2026-07-13 140306" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a478c455-dfae-4af0-a1fc-ce6d06458716" />

Employee well-being plays a crucial role in job satisfaction, productivity, and long-term retention. This page focuses on understanding the distribution of employees across different work-life balance categories and examining how attrition varies within each group.

By comparing the overall employee distribution with the number of employees who have left the organization, HR teams can identify whether work-life balance may be contributing to employee turnover and prioritize initiatives that improve employee satisfaction.

The dashboard includes:

- Active Employees by Work-Life Balance – Shows the distribution of active employees across different work-life balance categories.
- Attrition Count by Work-Life Balance – Highlights how employee attrition is distributed among these categories.

This analysis helps HR teams:

- Compare workforce distribution across work-life balance categories.
- Identify groups contributing most to attrition.
- Support initiatives aimed at improving employee well-being and retention.

### Travel Distance Analysis

<img width="869" height="489" alt="Screenshot 2026-07-08 123632" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/261bcb4f-7f04-4486-aa31-5194e84ae769" />

Commuting distance can influence an employee's overall job satisfaction and retention. This page examines the distribution of employees based on their distance from the workplace and analyzes how attrition varies across different distance ranges, helping HR identify whether commuting is a potential factor in employee turnover.

The dashboard includes:

- Active Employees by Distance from Home – Shows the distribution of active employees across different commuting distance ranges.
- Attrition Count by Distance from Home – Highlights attrition trends based on employees' distance from the workplace.

This analysis helps HR teams:

- Identify commuting patterns within the workforce.
- Evaluate the impact of travel distance on employee attrition.
- Support decisions related to flexible work policies and employee retention strategies.

### Employee Career Progression Analysis

<img width="865" height="486" alt="Screenshot 2026-07-08 123711" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0b5d14d5-c304-4086-a0b8-2bc75d44a913" />

Career growth and role stability are key factors in employee retention. This page analyzes employees based on their years in the current role and career mobility, helping HR understand how career progression relates to attrition and identify employees who may be at a higher risk of leaving.

The dashboard includes:

- Active Employees by Years in Current Role – Displays the distribution of employees based on the number of years spent in their current role.
- Attrition Count by Career Mobility – Shows how attrition varies across different levels of career mobility.

This analysis helps HR teams:

- Understand employee career progression patterns.
- Identify experience groups with higher attrition.
- Support workforce planning and internal career development initiatives.
